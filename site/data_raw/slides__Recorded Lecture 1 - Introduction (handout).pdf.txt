Divide-and-Conquer

---PAGE_BREAK---

Divide-and-Conquer paradigm
ВҲThe Divide-and-Conquer algorithm design paradigm works in 3
steps:
1. Divide the problem into smaller subproblems.
2. Conquer (i.e., solve) the smaller subproblems.
3. Combine the solutions of the smaller subproblems to obtain the
solution of the bigger problem.
ВҲAnalysing the time complexity of a divide-and-conquer algorithm
normally involves solving a recurrence relation.
ВҲNormally a polynomial time solution to the problem is already know,
and the divide-and-conquer strategy is used to reduce the time
complexity to a lower polynomial.

Space complexity
ВҲSpace complexity is the total amount of space taken by an algorithm
as a function of input size.
ВҲAuxiliary space complexity is the amount of space taken by an
algorithm excluding the space taken by the input .
в–¶Many textbooks and online resources do not distinguish between the above
two terms and use the term вҖңspace complexityвҖқ when they are in fact
referring to auxiliary space complexity.
в–¶In this unit, we use these two terms to differentiate between them.

In-place algorithm
ВҲAn in-place algorithm has O(1) auxiliary space complexity.
в–¶In other words, it only requires constant space in addition to the space
taken by its input.
в–¶Merging is not an in-place algorithm as it needs to create the output list
which is size n.
в–¶Be mindful that some books use a different definition (e.g., space taken by
recursion may be ignored). For the sake of this unit, we will use the above
definition.
